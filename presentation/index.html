The method

Simple on purpose

PEER RATIO = INVERTER ÷ PLANT MEDIAN (same timestamp)
Every five minutes, each inverter is divided by the plant median. Clouds, seasons and rain hit everyone at once and cancel out. 1.0 means healthy; below means trouble — this one just dropped to 0.55.

One trap: grid curtailment (EVU / DV) looks exactly like a fault, so we filter it explicitly — skip that and you flag healthy inverters all day.

The differentiator — limit + fix

We closed our own limitation

The peer ratio is relative, so a loss hitting every inverter equally would cancel out. So we also built an absolute twin: each inverter vs its own healthy baseline, from irradiance + module temperature. Predicts healthy output at R² 0.957.
